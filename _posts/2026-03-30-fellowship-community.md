One of the meaningful connections that has enriched our ministry here at The United Church is our relationship with The Fellowship Community (TFC). I wanted to take a moment in this newsletter to introduce or re-introduce you to this wonderful network, and share some exciting news about what's coming up.

The Fellowship Community is a national network of Presbyterian Church (USA) churches and leaders committed to living and proclaiming the good news of Jesus. Their mission is simple: gathering, equipping, and encouraging one another for healthy, missional, Christ-centered ministry. For us, this connection has been a wonderful way to deepen and enhance our Presbyterian roots, linking us with other PC(USA) pastors and congregations who share a common heart for the gospel and a commitment to faithful ministry.

What does TFC actually do? Quite a bit, it turns out. They host regional gatherings and monthly Zoom meetings where leaders can connect, learn, and pray together. They maintain a "Find a Call" network to help churches and ministry leaders find one another. They cultivate mission partnerships, including a current partnership with the Ukrainian Evangelical Theological Seminary, whose president Dr. Rusyn recently visited TFC congregations across the country to share how the church in Ukraine is faithfully being "salt and light" even amid the devastation of war. TFC is also currently engaged in a vision trip to Lahore, Pakistan, partnering with Friends of Forman College to see firsthand how the gospel is transforming young lives through education.

This spring, Christy and I will be attending the 2026 TFC National Gathering, April 27-30 in Zionsville, Indiana (near Indianapolis). The theme draws from Jeremiah 29:11: "For I know the plans I have for you." The gathering will feature speakers including Kara Powell, Scott Sunquist, Drew Hyun, and Mark Labberton, with workshops focused on younger and future leaders, family ministry, culture, and missional discipleship. We are genuinely looking forward to this time of renewal and connection with fellow Presbyterian leaders from across the country.

We'll look forward to sharing what we learn when we return. In the meantime, if you'd like to know more about The Fellowship Community, you can visit  [fellowship.community](https://fellowship.community/).
